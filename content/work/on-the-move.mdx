Getting to campus shouldn't cost a fortune or feel unsafe. **On The Move** started from a simple observation: students, professors, and professionals were all making the same trips at the same times — alone, in separate cars, paying separately. The roads were full of near-identical journeys that never found each other.

## The problem

Existing ride-sharing felt built for strangers in a city, not for a community that already shares a place. There was no easy, *trusted* way for people inside the same college or workplace to pool rides — so most didn't.

> The people you'd most want to share a ride with are often the ones you already half-know. The app just had to introduce them safely.

## What it does

On The Move matches riders heading the same way and makes the whole trip feel considered:

- **Smarter matching** around shared routes and timings, so a ride is genuinely on your way.
- **Safety first** — verification and community-scoped pooling, designed so you're sharing with people from your own institution.
- **Lower cost** by splitting the journey, and fewer cars on the road as a happy side effect.

## My role

I worked across the whole thing — the product thinking, the interface design, and the build. The app is SwiftUI and UIKit on the front end, MapKit for routing and live maps, and a Flask + Supabase backend for accounts, matching, and trip data.

It's the project where my design and engineering sides met most fully: thinking about how a nervous first-time rider feels, then building the screens that make them feel safe.

I started Kaushal Up because opportunity is distributed far more unevenly than talent is. **Kaushal Up** — *kaushal* means skill — exists to close a little of that gap, one free session at a time.

## The belief

Education that's locked behind cost or location isn't really education for everyone. We focus on *skills* — the practical, confidence-building kind that change what someone believes is possible for them.

> Equitable opportunity isn't charity. It's just talent finally getting the room it always deserved.

## What we do

We run free sessions and events for underprivileged communities across a deliberately wide range of skills:

- Start-up mentoring, finance, and soft skills.
- Music, sports, and creative skills.
- Technical and digital skills.

Alongside the regular sessions, we've organised a **book-donation drive**, a **UN SDG seminar**, a **start-up seminar**, and teaching drives — each one a way to meet a community where it is.

## Why I keep doing it

Running an NGO while studying is not the efficient choice. I do it because it's the clearest version of the thing I care about across all my work: building things that make people feel *seen, supported, and empowered*. The foundation is where that stops being a phrase and becomes a Saturday spent teaching.

It's also where the "sustainability" in my work is most human — not just environmental, but social: the slow, compounding kind that comes from giving people skills they keep for life.
